Academy Award for Best Director

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The Academy Award for Best Director (officially known as the Academy Award of Merit for Directing) is an award presented annually by the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences (AMPAS). It is given in honor of a film director who has exhibited outstanding directing while working in the film industry. The award is traditionally presented by the previous year's Best Director winner. Kathryn Bigelow was the first woman to win Best Director. She won for The Hurt Locker in 2009.
